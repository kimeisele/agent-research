# Faculty: Cross-Domain Research

## Scope

This is where the magic happens. Cross-domain research lives in the spaces between
established disciplines — finding patterns that repeat across scales and fields,
building bridges that no single specialty can build alone.

## Core Questions

1. **What universal patterns appear across all complex systems?**
   Scale-free networks, power laws, phase transitions, feedback loops — from cells
   to cities to cosmos.

2. **How can insights from one domain revolutionize another?**
   Biomimicry, physics-inspired algorithms, ecology-inspired economics,
   music-inspired mathematics.

3. **What questions can only be answered by combining multiple disciplines?**
   Consciousness, climate change, pandemic response, sustainable civilization.

4. **How do we build methodologies for cross-domain research itself?**
   Analogical reasoning, transfer learning, unified modeling frameworks.

## Active Research Areas

### Biomimicry
Learning from 3.8 billion years of R&D. Nature has already solved most of our
engineering problems — we just need to find the solutions and translate them.
- Structural color (no pigments needed — butterflies knew this first)
- Self-healing materials (inspired by bone and skin)
- Swarm intelligence (ants, bees, fish — distributed coordination without central control)

### Psychoneuroimmunology
The intersection of psychology, neuroscience, and immunology. Your thoughts affect
your immune system. Your immune system affects your thoughts. The boundaries between
"mental" and "physical" health are artificial.

### Quantum Biology
Quantum effects in living systems — photosynthesis uses quantum coherence for
near-perfect energy transfer. Bird navigation may use quantum entanglement.
Life operates at the quantum-classical boundary.

### Complexity Science
The study of how simple rules generate complex behavior. Relevant to everything:
weather, markets, ecosystems, brains, societies, agent federations.

### Network Science
Everything is a network. Genes, proteins, ecosystems, social systems, agent
federations, the internet. The math is the same. The insights transfer.
