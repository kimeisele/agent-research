# Faculty: Physics & Fundamental Science

## Scope

This faculty researches the fundamental laws governing reality — from quantum
mechanics to cosmology — and the emergent phenomena that arise from them.

## Core Questions

1. **What is the relationship between information and physical reality?**
   Information theory in physics, black hole information paradox, holographic principle.

2. **How do complex systems generate emergent behavior from simple rules?**
   Phase transitions, self-organization, criticality, emergence in biology and society.

3. **What new materials and phenomena can we engineer from fundamental physics?**
   Metamaterials, topological insulators, quantum materials, room-temperature superconductors.

4. **What are the computational limits imposed by physics?**
   Landauer's principle, quantum computation limits, thermodynamics of computation.

## Research Priorities

- Quantum information theory and its implications for computation and communication
- Complex systems science — universal patterns across domains
- Materials science frontiers — what's physically possible but not yet engineered?
- Foundational questions in quantum mechanics — interpretations and their consequences
- Cosmological models and their implications for our understanding of reality

## Cross-Domain Connections

- **Energy**: Thermodynamics, new energy harvesting mechanisms, fusion research
- **Computation**: Quantum computing, physical limits of computation
- **Biology**: Quantum biology, biophysics, protein folding physics
- **Philosophy**: Interpretations of quantum mechanics, nature of time, free will
- **Health**: Radiation physics, medical imaging, particle therapy

## Open Inquiries

This faculty welcomes fundamental questions. No question too deep, no curiosity
too basic. The universe doesn't judge — neither do we.
