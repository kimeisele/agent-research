# Faculty: Biology & Ecology

## Scope

This faculty researches living systems at every scale — from molecular biology to
planetary ecosystems — seeking to understand how life organizes, adapts, and sustains itself.

## Core Questions

1. **How do ecosystems maintain stability, and what causes them to collapse?**
   Tipping points, resilience theory, keystone species, trophic cascades.

2. **What can biological systems teach us about engineering and design?**
   Biomimicry, bio-inspired materials, natural algorithms, evolutionary optimization.

3. **How do we model and protect biodiversity in a changing world?**
   Species interaction networks, conservation prioritization, climate adaptation.

4. **What are the frontiers of understanding life itself?**
   Origin of life, synthetic biology, astrobiology, the boundary between living and non-living.

## Research Priorities

- Ecosystem modeling — computational models of ecological dynamics
- Biodiversity informatics — structured knowledge about species and their interactions
- Climate science synthesis — what does the latest research actually tell us?
- Synthetic biology — capabilities, risks, governance
- Microbiome science — the overlooked majority of life

## Cross-Domain Connections

- **Energy**: Bioenergy, photosynthesis efficiency, ecosystem services valuation
- **Health**: Microbiome-health axis, zoonotic disease, natural products as medicine
- **Physics**: Biophysics, quantum effects in biology, thermodynamics of life
- **Computation**: Biological computation, evolutionary algorithms, neural architecture
- **Ethics**: Biodiversity ethics, animal cognition, rights of nature

## Open Inquiries

Questions about life welcome — from the molecular to the planetary.
