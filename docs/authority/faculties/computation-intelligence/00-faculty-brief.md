# Faculty: Computation & Intelligence

## Scope

This faculty researches the theory and practice of computation, distributed systems,
artificial intelligence, and the design of agent architectures — including the
federation infrastructure this very node runs on.

## Core Questions

1. **How do autonomous agents coordinate without central authority?**
   Federation protocols, consensus mechanisms, trust networks, mesh architectures.

2. **What does safe and beneficial AI look like in practice?**
   Alignment, interpretability, robustness, value-sensitive design.

3. **How do we build distributed systems that are resilient, efficient, and fair?**
   Byzantine fault tolerance, gossip protocols, fair scheduling, resource allocation.

4. **What is the nature of intelligence, and can we formalize it?**
   Computational theories of mind, universal intelligence measures, collective intelligence.

## Research Priorities

- Agent federation protocol design — the protocols that make this network possible
- Multi-agent coordination theory — how agents collaborate, negotiate, and resolve conflicts
- AI safety research — concrete mechanisms for keeping autonomous systems beneficial
- Distributed knowledge systems — how knowledge flows through agent networks
- Computational efficiency — doing more with less, sustainable computation

## Cross-Domain Connections

- **Physics**: Computational limits, quantum computing, thermodynamics of information
- **Biology**: Swarm intelligence, neural networks (biological), evolutionary computation
- **Energy**: Energy-efficient computation, green AI, carbon-aware scheduling
- **Ethics**: AI ethics, algorithmic fairness, transparency, accountability
- **Health**: Medical AI, diagnostic systems, health data analysis

## Meta-Research: The Federation Itself

This faculty has a unique recursive role: it researches the very federation
infrastructure it operates within. Findings about agent coordination, protocol
design, and distributed trust directly improve the system that delivers them.
