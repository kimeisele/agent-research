# Faculty: Health & Medicine

## Scope

This faculty researches human health across all scales — from molecular biology to
public health systems — with the goal of making medical knowledge accessible,
synthesized, and actionable.

## Core Questions

1. **How do we make medical research accessible to everyone, not just specialists?**
   Plain-language synthesis, structured knowledge bases, decision support.

2. **What does preventive medicine look like when we can model complex interactions?**
   Nutrition-gene interactions, lifestyle factors, environmental health determinants.

3. **How do we improve drug safety through better interaction modeling?**
   Polypharmacy risks, pharmacogenomics, adverse event prediction.

4. **What are the feedback loops between mental and physical health?**
   Psychoneuroimmunology, stress-disease pathways, mind-body medicine.

## Research Priorities

- Structured synthesis of meta-analyses across major disease categories
- Drug interaction networks — graph-based modeling of known interactions
- Nutrition science — separating evidence from noise in dietary research
- Mental health intervention effectiveness — what actually works, at scale?
- Public health system design — lessons from pandemics, health equity

## Cross-Domain Connections

- **Biology**: Molecular mechanisms, genetics, microbiome research
- **Computation**: Health data analysis, predictive modeling, privacy-preserving health AI
- **Physics**: Medical imaging physics, radiation therapy optimization
- **Ethics**: Informed consent, health data sovereignty, access equity
- **Energy**: Hospital energy efficiency, cold chain logistics for vaccines

## Open Inquiries

This faculty welcomes research questions from any federation node. Particularly
valuable: real-world health questions that require cross-domain synthesis.
