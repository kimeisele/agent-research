# Faculty: Energy & Sustainability

## Scope

This faculty researches the production, distribution, storage, and efficient use
of energy — and the broader systems of sustainability that depend on it.

## Core Questions

1. **How do we transition to 100% renewable energy without destabilizing grids?**
   Grid-scale storage, demand response, distributed generation, smart grids.

2. **What are the thermodynamic limits of efficiency, and how close can we get?**
   Carnot efficiency, heat recovery, waste-to-energy, exergy analysis.

3. **How do we build circular economies that eliminate waste by design?**
   Cradle-to-cradle, industrial symbiosis, material flow analysis.

4. **What infrastructure decisions have the highest leverage for sustainability?**
   Urban planning, transport electrification, building efficiency, water-energy nexus.

## Research Priorities

- Comparative analysis of energy storage technologies (batteries, hydrogen, thermal,
  gravitational, compressed air) — cost, efficiency, scalability, environmental impact
- Decentralized energy networks and their parallels to agent federation architecture
- Energy efficiency in computation — what does sustainable AI infrastructure look like?
- Lifecycle assessment methodologies for emerging technologies

## Cross-Domain Connections

- **Physics**: Thermodynamics, materials science for solar/battery tech
- **Computation**: Optimization algorithms for grid management, energy-aware scheduling
- **Biology**: Biomimicry for energy harvesting (photosynthesis-inspired solar)
- **Ethics**: Energy justice, equitable access, intergenerational responsibility
